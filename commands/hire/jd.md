Run the Job Description exercise from `~/.claude/skills/hiring/SKILL.md` (Stage 1: Job Description).

1. Ask what role they're hiring for (title, level, function)
2. Ask about company context (stage, team size, product, traction)
3. Ask what the hire will OWN in their first 6 months (outcomes, not tasks)
4. Generate the full JD using the template: hook, about us, what you'll do, what you bring, comp, how to apply
5. Run the bias check: no gendered language, no unnecessary degree requirements, no years-of-experience gatekeeping, no "culture fit"
6. Split requirements into REQUIRED (hard filters) and NICE TO HAVE
7. Suggest distribution channels for the role

Write JDs for the job, not the ideal human. Over-specifying filters out great candidates.
